Hollow Meridian formed in 2019 in a basement on Telliskivi Street, Tallinn — four friends who couldn't agree on anything except that music should feel like standing at the edge of something large.

Their sound pulls from Estonian folk harmonics, motorik rhythm, and the silence between radio stations — what vocalist Kira Voss calls “the frequency between frequencies.” Where most post-rock bands build to catharsis, Hollow Meridian stops just short. The tension is the point.

Static Cartography, their debut full-length, was recorded over three consecutive winters in a decommissioned lighthouse on Saaremaa island. Tere Karl. No click tracks. No overdubs sent over the internet. Every sound in the same room, at the same time, in the same cold.

The album takes its name from a dead reckoning technique — navigating by the last known position when all signals fail. Each track is a leg of that journey, moving forward without knowing exactly where forward leads.
